# National Fentanyl Operations Center (NFOC) Proposal

## Governance Structure for Mandatory Interagency Cooperation

---

## Precedent

The National Counterterrorism Center (NCTC) was created after 9/11 to solve the interagency intelligence-sharing failures that allowed the attacks. Agencies had the data to prevent 9/11. They didn't share it.

Fentanyl kills more Americans every year than every terrorist attack in U.S. history combined. The agencies have the data to disrupt fentanyl. They don't share it. The precedent is established.

## Structure

- **Authority:** Housed under ONDCP (White House). Not subordinate to any single department.
- **Leadership:** Director appointed by the President, confirmed by the Senate.
- **Deputies:** Deputy directors from DEA, FBI, DHS, Treasury, DOD.
- **Staffing:** Detailees from all participating agencies (same model as NCTC).
- **Budget:** Authority over counter-fentanyl appropriations. Agencies receive funding through NFOC based on participation and contribution metrics.

## Key Authorities

1. **Mandate data contribution** from all participating agencies. No opt-out.
2. **De-conflict operations** across agencies and jurisdictions automatically.
3. **Direct joint operations** (quarterly SURGE OPS targeting highest-priority dealers).
4. **Annual report to Congress** with CRI-based outcome metrics.

## Why This Solves the Problem

Interagency cooperation fails because agencies have no incentive to share and every incentive to hoard. Data is power. Sharing data means sharing credit. No bureaucracy voluntarily surrenders power.

The NFOC solves this by making participation in the unified system the only pathway to dedicated counter-fentanyl funding. You don't share, you don't get paid.

## Establishment

Could be established via:
- **Executive order** (fastest, presidential authority, but vulnerable to future administration reversal)
- **Statutory authorization** (permanent, requires legislation, stronger foundation)

Recommended: Executive order for immediate establishment, followed by statutory authorization for permanence.
