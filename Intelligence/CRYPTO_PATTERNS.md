## Key Findings (Public Sources)

- 97% of Chinese precursor manufacturers identified by TRM Labs accept cryptocurrency.
- Crypto payments to Chinese precursor manufacturers increased 600% from 2022 to 2023, reaching over $26 million.
- Payments more than doubled again in the first four months of 2024.
- One group of suspected China-based chemical traders received $37.8M in crypto from 2018-2023.
- TRM Labs estimates cartel-associated money launderers moved $3 billion+ on-chain as of January 2026, mostly in the past two years.
- DEA seized $2.5B in crypto from 2020-2024, surpassing $2.2B in cash seizures for the first time.

## Laundering Methods

**Cash-to-crypto pipeline:**
1. Cartel affiliates collect bulk cash from U.S. drug sales.
2. Cash delivered to Chinese brokers in U.S. cities.
3. Brokers convert to cryptocurrency.
4. Crypto transferred globally or used to purchase precursors directly.

**On-chain obfuscation:**
- Funds move through pass-through addresses, consolidation nodes, and intermediary services.
- Flows intersect with decentralized exchanges, cross-chain bridges, and liquidity providers.
- "Chain-hops" move funds through multiple intermediate wallets.
- Mixers used for layering (but less sophisticated than nation-state actors).

**Key vulnerability:** Cartel-affiliated launderers operate unsophisticatedly compared to North Korean or Russian state-backed cyber actors. They move funds swiftly through centralized accounts and unhosted wallets with limited obfuscation. Pattern is detectable.

## Tracing Capability

Commercial blockchain analytics firms (Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic) can:
- Cluster wallets controlled by the same entity
- Trace transaction flows from street-level payments to precursor suppliers
- Identify when funds pass through sanctioned entities or known cartel addresses
- Map the complete financial chain from U.S. drug cash to Chinese factory

The payment trail from street sale to precursor purchase is entirely on-chain and traceable with existing commercial tools.
