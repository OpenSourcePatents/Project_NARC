## Text Obfuscation Methods

Dealers also bypass keyword detection by inserting punctuation into drug names:
- "s"h:r'o,o'm:s" = shrooms
- "d:m,t" = DMT
- "pilz" = pills
- "Xanaz" = Xanax
- "pase" = cocaine (Spanish slang)

## Dealer Operational Patterns

Beyond specific codes, dealers follow identifiable behavioral patterns that are more durable than any specific emoji:

1. **Post-delete cycle.** Product advertisements posted in stories or feeds, then deleted within hours after initial contacts made.
2. **Disappearing messages.** Conversations moved to Snapchat (auto-delete) or Signal/Telegram (encrypted, ephemeral).
3. **DM redirect.** Initial contact on one platform, transaction conducted on a different, more private platform.
4. **Payment app linking.** Cash App/Venmo/Zelle usernames shared in DMs for payment.
5. **Account migration.** After ban, new account created within hours with similar username patterns and the same payment app links.
6. **Cross-platform presence.** Same dealer operating on Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, and Telegram simultaneously.
7. **Geographic tagging.** Location tags or area codes used to signal service territory.

## Key Insight for SOCIAL SCAN

Current platform moderation uses keyword matching and basic image recognition. It catches specific words and symbols.

SOCIAL SCAN uses behavioral pattern analysis. It identifies dealers by HOW they operate, not just what words they use. A dealer who changes their emoji code every week is still identifiable by their post-delete cycle, DM redirect behavior, payment app linking, and account migration pattern.

The behavior is the signature, not the symbol.

- CBS News investigation: Fake 18-year-old profile found a dealer within 48 hours.
- Snapchat drug transactions linked to fentanyl deaths in at least 19 states.
- 42%+ of counterfeit pills contain lethal fentanyl doses.
- No legitimate pharmaceutical pill is sold on social media. Every social media pill purchase is counterfeit by definition.
