## The Six-Stage Closed Loop

The fentanyl economy operates as a closed financial cycle. Every stage has exploitable data signatures.

### Stage 1: Precursor Procurement
Chinese chemical companies sell precursors to Mexican cartels. 97% accept cryptocurrency. Precursors with no legitimate industrial use (ANPP, benzylfentanyl, 4-AP, 4-piperidone, norfentanyl) plus dual-use reagents (propionyl chloride, sodium borohydride, phenethyl bromide, NPP).

### Stage 2: Shipping
China to Mexico/U.S. via mislabeled freight, postal services, express carriers. Disguised as furniture parts, vases, makeup, nuts, dog food, motor oil. False labels, fraudulent postage, re-shippers.

### Stage 3: Synthesis
Clandestine labs in Mexico (Sinaloa, Jalisco) and increasingly in the U.S. Kitchen-sized labs produce millions of doses. 1,760 labs destroyed under Sheinbaum through Dec 2025.

### Stage 4: Distribution and Street Sales
Crosses border primarily through legal ports of entry in legitimate freight. Street sales generate bulk cash in CA, FL, NY, TX.

### Stage 5: Money Laundering (Critical Node)
1. Cartel associates deliver bulk drug cash to Chinese brokers in U.S. cities (LA, NYC, Miami).
2. Chinese brokers convert cash to cryptocurrency (BTC ~60%, USDT/Tron ~30%, USDT/ETH ~6%).
3. Crypto layered through mixers, chain-hops, intermediate wallets.
4. Simultaneously, Chinese associates in Guangzhou buy consumer electronics.
5. Electronics shipped to Mexico at below-market prices.
6. Mexican businesses sell goods, generating clean cash paid to cartels.

**Why Chinese brokers participate:** China restricts foreign currency purchases to $50,000/year. Underground banking via cartel cash gives wealthy Chinese nationals access to unrestricted USD. Both sides benefit.

### Stage 6: Reinvestment
Clean funds purchase more precursors. Cycle repeats.

## Scale

- TRM Labs: $3 billion+ moved on-chain by cartel-associated launderers as of January 2026.
- Crypto payments to Chinese precursor manufacturers: 600% increase 2022-2023.
- One China-based chemical trader group: $37.8M in crypto (2018-2023).
- From 2020-2024: DEA seized $2.5B in crypto vs. $2.2B in cash (crypto surpassed cash).
- Sinaloa cartel laundered $50M+ through Chinese underground exchanges (2019-2023 per DOJ).

## The Vulnerability

Unlike nation-state cyber actors (North Korean crypto theft), cartel-affiliated launderers are technically unsophisticated. They move funds through centralized exchanges, unhosted wallets, and identifiable blockchain patterns. They advertise precursors on open e-commerce platforms. They accept payment through traceable cryptocurrency.

This is not a hard intelligence problem. It is an integration problem. The data is visible. Nobody is connecting it.
