# Mexico Cartel Status

## Current Landscape Post-El Mencho (as of March 2026)

---

## El Mencho Operation (February 22, 2026)

Mexican special operations forces, acting on CIA intelligence via the Joint Interagency Task Force-Counter Cartel (JITC-CC), raided a gated community in Tapalpa, Jalisco. CJNG leader Nemesio "El Mencho" Oseguera Cervantes was wounded during the engagement and died in transit to Mexico City.

Within hours, CJNG launched coordinated retaliatory violence across approximately 20 states: road blockades, vehicle arson, shootings, attacks on civilian infrastructure. Guadalajara was shut down. Schools cancelled. Foreign governments issued shelter-in-place advisories.

The violence was intense but brief. Most incidents ended within hours. Analysts described it as a "stress response" demonstrating reach but not sustained capability.

## Current Cartel Landscape

### CJNG (Jalisco New Generation Cartel)
- Leader killed. Succession uncertain.
- Heavily militarized, most violent cartel, special operations capability.
- Dominant in fentanyl and methamphetamine trafficking.
- Expanded aggressively during Sinaloa civil war.
- Key question: clean succession or fragmentation?

### Sinaloa Cartel
- In active civil war since Zambada arrest (2024).
- Chapitos faction vs. Mayo loyalists.
- Culiacan turned into war zone. Homicide rate increased 400%.
- Still operational for drug trafficking despite internal conflict.

### Other Organizations
- Gulf Cartel: fragmented, multiple factions, primarily Texas corridor.
- Various smaller organizations filling gaps from fragmentation.
- Number of active criminal organizations has grown from 2 (2005) to 23+ (2023).

## Sheinbaum Administration Posture

President Claudia Sheinbaum has shifted from predecessor AMLO's "hugs not bullets" approach to more confrontational posture. Through December 2025: 39,000 arrests, 20,000 guns seized, 1,760 drug labs destroyed (government claims). Sheinbaum claims 40% homicide reduction from September 2024 to December 2025 (analysts dispute these numbers).

## U.S.-Mexico Cooperation

- Trump administration designated CJNG and Sinaloa as Foreign Terrorist Organizations (February 2025).
- U.S. provided intelligence support for El Mencho operation via JITC-CC and CIA.
- Trump administration expanded covert drone program (started under Biden) to identify fentanyl labs in Mexico.
- Targeted strikes on alleged drug trafficking vessels in Caribbean linked to Tren de Aragua.
- Tariffs imposed on Mexican imports over failure to curb immigration and drug trafficking.

## Implications for Total Kill Chain

The killing of El Mencho reinforces the core thesis: kingpin strategy does not disrupt supply chains. The fentanyl pipeline from China through Mexico to U.S. streets operates independently of any individual leader. Structural disruption (financial, precursor, distribution) is the only approach with potential for lasting impact.
