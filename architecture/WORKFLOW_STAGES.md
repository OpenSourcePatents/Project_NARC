# Workflow Stages

## Seven-Stage Common Workflow: From Detection to Prosecution

---

Any agency can enter at any stage. Every action generates intelligence that feeds forward and backward to all other stages.

```
STAGE 1: EARLY WARNING (NARC)
    |
STAGE 2: PRECURSOR DETECTION
    |
STAGE 3: SHIPMENT INTERDICTION
    |
STAGE 4: FINANCIAL DISRUPTION
    |
STAGE 5: DISTRIBUTION NETWORK MAPPING
    |
STAGE 6: DEALER IDENTIFICATION AND CASE BUILDING
    |
STAGE 7: INTELLIGENCE FEEDBACK
    |
[LOOP BACK TO ALL STAGES]
```

---

## Stage 1: Early Warning (NARC)

**Lead:** ONDCP/HIDTA, CDC
**Supporting:** State health departments, EMS, medical examiners

**What happens:** NARC detects emerging crisis zones via Crisis Rate Index (CRI) alerting. Overdose events are aggregated from ODMAP, EMS naloxone logs, 911 dispatch, medical examiners, and CDC SUDORS. Data is normalized against census population and evaluated against threshold tables.

**Alert tiers:**
- YELLOW -- Elevated. CRI above baseline. Pattern developing.
- ORANGE -- Sustained. CRI above threshold for sustained period or accelerating.
- RED -- Critical. Opioid emergency by any reasonable definition.

**Output to system:** Geographic hotspot identification triggers enhanced monitoring across all downstream stages. CRI trends become the primary effectiveness metric: are deaths going down in targeted areas?

**Who benefits:** Every downstream stage. NARC is the system's eyes.

---

## Stage 2: Precursor Detection

**Lead:** DEA Diversion Control / OFAC
**Supporting:** HSI, CBP, State Department INL, INCB

**What happens:** PRECURSOR WATCH identifies Chinese chemical companies advertising fentanyl precursors. Cross-references against OFAC SDN list, DEA indictments, SSL chemicals. New entities flagged for investigation or sanctions designation.

**Output to system:** New precursor seller entities, associated wallet addresses, shipping patterns.

**Who benefits:** CBP (knows what to intercept), FinCEN (knows what financial patterns to flag), HSI (investigation targets), State (diplomatic leverage).

---

## Stage 3: Shipment Interdiction

**Lead:** CBP (ports of entry) / USPIS (mail)
**Supporting:** DEA, HSI, FDA

**What happens:** Import records and mail screening cross-referenced against PRECURSOR WATCH entity database. Anomalous shipments from known precursor-producing regions flagged. Seized shipments enter chain of custody for prosecution.

**Output to system:** Seizure records, shipping routes, intermediary addresses, lab analysis results.

**Who benefits:** DEA (supply chain mapping), HSI (transnational investigation leads), NFLIS (chemical signatures), TRADE TRAP (shipping pattern analysis).

---

## Stage 4: Financial Disruption

**Lead:** FinCEN / OFAC / IRS-CI
**Supporting:** DEA, FBI, HSI

**What happens:** CHAIN TRACE and TRADE TRAP analyze crypto flows and trade-based laundering. BANK WATCH scores financial institutions for fentanyl corridor exposure. FinCEN advisories updated. OFAC designations expanded.

**Output to system:** Frozen assets, designated wallets, sanctioned entities, financial institution risk scores, money laundering network maps.

**Who benefits:** Everyone. Financial intelligence is the connective tissue between precursor suppliers and street dealers.

---

## Stage 5: Distribution Network Mapping

**Lead:** DEA SOD / EPIC
**Supporting:** HIDTA ISCs, FBI, HSI, state fusion centers

**What happens:** OD MAP data, NFLIS batch signatures, financial flow analysis, and social media intelligence combined to map distribution networks from wholesale to retail. Identifies mid-level distributors connecting cartel logistics to street-level dealers. This is the layer most invisible under current operations.

**Output to system:** Network maps showing supplier-distributor-dealer relationships, territorial boundaries, supply chain nodes.

**Who benefits:** DEA field divisions (targeting), HIDTA task forces (operations), U.S. Attorneys (conspiracy charges), state/local LE (enforcement).

---

## Stage 6: Dealer Identification and Case Building

**Lead:** DEA OD Justice Task Force / HIDTA Task Forces / FBI JCODE
**Supporting:** Local PD, county sheriffs, state police, U.S. Attorneys, state AGs

**What happens:** SOCIAL SCAN, DARK WATCH, PAYMENT TRACE, OD MAP, and IDENTITY FUSION identify and resolve dealers. CASE BUILDER assembles prosecution packages. Priority scoring ranks targets.

**Prosecution routing:**
- Linked fatalities + sufficient evidence = Federal (21 U.S.C. 841(b)(1)(C), 20-year mandatory minimum)
- Distribution to minors = Federal (enhanced penalties)
- Conspiracy + network value = Federal (21 U.S.C. 846)
- Possession with intent, lower volume = State prosecution
- Financial crimes component = Add IRS-CI, pursue money laundering charges in parallel

**Local Agency On-Ramp:** Any officer entering an OD event in ODMAP and preserving victim phone data has contributed targeting intelligence to the national system. A county deputy in rural New Hampshire generates the same quality of input as a DEA special agent in Los Angeles.

**Output to system:** Resolved dealer profiles, prosecution packages, arrest warrants, case dispositions.

---

## Stage 7: Intelligence Feedback

**Lead:** All participating agencies

**What happens:** Every arrest, prosecution, and seizure generates new intelligence that feeds back into all prior stages. Dealer interviews provide supply chain information. Seized devices yield new digital identities, wallet addresses, and communication records. Toxicology data refines batch signature clustering.

**Output to system:** New targets at every level of the supply chain.

**The feedback loop:** NARC closes the outer loop. After enforcement actions, NARC measures whether CRI is declining in targeted areas. If deaths drop, the disruption worked. If not, the supply chain rerouted and targeting needs to shift. CRI is the scoreboard.
